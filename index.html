Aquaculture

What is Aquaculture?

Aquaculture is the farming of fish, crustaceans, mollusks, aquatic plants, algae, and other organisms. It involves cultivating freshwater and saltwater populations under controlled conditions.

It plays a crucial role in providing sustainable seafood, reducing pressure on wild fish stocks, and supporting livelihoods.

Explore more about aquaculture and its significance in our ongoing research.

Aquaculture Techniques

Various techniques are used in aquaculture, including pond systems, cage systems, and recirculating systems. These methods differ depending on the species being cultured and environmental considerations.

Our research focuses on evaluating different aquaculture techniques to enhance sustainability and productivity.

Challenges in Aquaculture

Aquaculture faces challenges such as disease outbreaks, environmental impacts, and socio-economic issues. Addressing these challenges is crucial for the long-term sustainability of the industry.

Learn more about the challenges and innovative solutions in our research findings.
